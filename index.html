The Disciple’s Journey

Jesus awakens our hearts and invites us to walk in His way, leaving behind our old lives to discover true belonging and purpose.

“Come, follow me,” Jesus said, “and I will send you out to fish for people.” — Matthew 4:19

Our hearts are stirred to recognize His presence and call. Jesus meets us where we are and awakens faith in us.

John 1:35–39

Following Jesus requires trust — a willingness to step out from what’s familiar and surrender our own agenda to His will.

Luke 5:11

We learn to listen to His voice — through Scripture, prayer, and the Spirit’s prompting — discovering that following begins with hearing.

John 10:27

Jesus shapes our inner life as we learn to abide in Him, transforming our desires, habits, and character.

He appointed twelve that they might be with him. — Mark 3:14

Abiding in Christ roots us in love. As we spend time with Him, we discover that formation happens through relationship, not performance.

John 15:4–5

Christ forms our inner life, shaping how we respond, speak, and love others. Our integrity becomes an overflow of His presence.

Romans 12:2

Through prayer, solitude, fasting, and Scripture meditation, we are reshaped by grace and made ready for His mission.

Matthew 6:6

Jesus sends us into the world to reflect His love, proclaim the good news, and bring restoration to others.

The Lord appointed seventy-two others and sent them two by two ahead of him. — Luke 10:1–3

Disciples serve with compassion, reflecting the heart of Christ in tangible acts of love and justice.

Galatians 5:13

We are witnesses of God’s grace — bringing hope, truth, and reconciliation wherever we go.

Romans 10:14–15

We grow as leaders when we take responsibility for others’ growth and guide them toward the way of Jesus.

2 Timothy 2:2

The life of Jesus multiplies through us as we disciple others, investing in the next generation of Kingdom leaders.

Therefore go and make disciples of all nations. — Matthew 28:19–20

We teach others what Jesus has taught us — helping them discover their gifts and calling.

Ephesians 4:11–13

Discipleship is not for the elite — it’s for everyone who follows Jesus, empowered by the Holy Spirit.

Acts 1:8

We intentionally develop others to become disciple-makers who carry the movement forward.

2 Timothy 2:2

We live for God’s glory, bearing lasting fruit that continues to bless others long after we are gone.

This is to my Father’s glory, that you bear much fruit. — John 15:8

Our ultimate calling is to abide in Christ. Fruitfulness is not achieved, but received through His life in us.

John 15:5

As we release others into mission, the Gospel spreads through generations of faithful disciples.

Colossians 1:6

All of life becomes worship — work, relationships, and rest flow from a heart centered on God’s glory.

1 Corinthians 10:31
